================================================================================
TATVA — ENTERPRISE PLM FOR PROCESS INDUSTRIES
90-SECOND MARKETING VIDEO VOICEOVER SCRIPT
================================================================================
Version: 1.0
Duration: 90 seconds
Target Audience: VP of R&D, Head of Regulatory Affairs, CTO, COO at food &
  beverage, CPG, chemical, polymer, paint, and rubber manufacturers.
Tone: Warm, confident, forward-leaning — Apple Keynote meets Salesforce Dreamforce.
  No hyperbole. No filler. Every word earns its place.

================================================================================
SUGGESTED AI VOICE (ElevenLabs)
================================================================================
Voice: "Adam" (deep, warm, authoritative American male)
  OR:  "Josh" (upbeat, clear, slightly younger energy — good for SaaS demos)
Stability: 0.55
Clarity + Similarity: 0.80
Style Exaggeration: 0.15
Speaking Rate: Slightly slower than default (-5 to -10%)
  Rationale: Give each line room to breathe; this is cinematic, not a radio ad.

Alternative: ElevenLabs "Daniel" (British-ish neutral, also works well for
  enterprise SaaS if the target market skews international).

================================================================================
BACKGROUND MUSIC
================================================================================
Track Style: Cinematic, building electronic/orchestral hybrid
  Think: Hans Zimmer "Time" (Inception) energy, but shorter and more modern.
  OR: Something in the style of "Interstellar" ambient beds with a hopeful
  uplift in the final 15 seconds.
Suggested Sources:
  - Artlist.io: search "cinematic corporate uplift"
  - Epidemic Sound: "Confident Horizon" or "Tech Forward" playlists
  - Musicbed: search "Apple ad documentary"
Volume Mix: Music at 15-20% under voiceover; swell to 40% during the visual-only

================================================================================
DIRECTOR / EDITOR NOTES
================================================================================

PACING:
  - Scenes 1 through 2 are slow and cinematic. Let visuals breathe.
  - Scenes 3 through 6 are the product deep-dive — pace is brisker, confident.
  - Scenes 7 and 8 are punchy one-liners. Short. Sharp.
  - Scene 9 is the close — pull back to contemplative pace, then a firm, clean
    call to action.

EMPHASIS WORDS (bold in recording sessions):
  "Tatva" — always with weight and warmth; it's the brand name.
  "automatically" — this word is gold for operations-focused buyers.
  "under ten minutes" — surprising, memorable; inflect upward slightly.
  "in one place" — resolution, relief; slow down here.
  "always" (re: data) — firm and protective; enterprise buyers care about this.
  "Made in India — for the world" — pride without chest-thumping.

BREATH MARKS (suggested natural pauses beyond those noted above):
  After "Meet Tatva." — 1 full second.
  After "They deserve Tatva." — 1.5 seconds before URL.
  After "No rip-and-replace." — half beat.

RECORDING SETUP:
  - Dry studio or treated room. No reverb on the voice.
  - Ask the voice artist for 2-3 takes of the full script.
  - Get isolated pickups of: "Meet Tatva.", "under ten minutes.",
    "They deserve Tatva.", and "tatva.io — get started today."
  - Pickups allow fine-tuning the edit without a full re-record.
